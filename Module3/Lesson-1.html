Introduction to Reel Injun:

Reel Injun is a 2009 Canadian documentary directed by Cree filmmaker Neil Diamond, who is from Waskaganish, Quebec. The film takes viewers on a journey that spans a century of cinema and provides an insightful look at representations of Indigenous people in film. You’ll have a chance to examine these representations from historical and present-day perspectives as well as from Indigenous and non-Indigenous perspectives.

You will likely note that the concept of “the gaze” is introduced in Reel Injun. The media concept of “the gaze,” which is related to notions of power and oppression, describes how media content is made to please or entertain, and match the perspectives of, members of the dominant culture. For example, the male gaze serves up archetypical representations of women as provocative or motherly; these fit a male definition of how a woman should be or act. The white gaze serves up many representations of racialized people that serve white dominant cultural notions of how Black, Indigenous, and People of Colour should be or act.
