Introduction to Module 3

Indigenous Peoples’ lives have been infiltrated by dominant settler culture, which has created the perception that Indigenous Knowledges and Peoples are inferior. There were assimilative polices that would force Indigenous people and communities to integrate into Canadian society and accept Christian worldviews. Today, Indigenous communities are reclaiming knowledges, cultures, and languages through the process of decolonization. Breaking down barriers and stereotypes is part of this process.

This module will introduce concepts of power, privilege, and oppression. Its objective is to show how these perpetuate colonial narratives and affect how Indigenous Peoples are portrayed in the media. You will also begin to consider the ongoing impact of policies and laws that only apply to Indigenous Nations.

In this module, you will be introduced to the movie, Reel Injun. Two other media pieces in this course tell a similar story of the arc of challenges and growth experienced by Indigenous Peoples. While Reel Injun examines the film industry, Rumble: The Indians Who Rocked the World looks at the music industry, and CBC’s 7th Indigenous Speaker Series presentation by Connie Walker explores journalism.
