Module 1: Creation Story

Summary of content explored and self-assessment.

Objectives

  • Describe the significance of Indigenous storytelling.
  • Apply concepts of responsibility to community development.
  • ‘Apply’ the code of ethics based on the Anishnaabe Seven Grandfather Teachings.
  • Relate examples of oral tradition of Indigenous people in relation to the land.
  • Investigate the significance of traditional ecological knowledge.

Summary

Throughout the four different lessons, you engaged with this module (week?) you have explored Indigenous storytelling and narratives. You learned about different types of Indigenous storytelling (acimoona & tipachimoona).

You read about an Indigenous Creation Story and reflected on the impact Creation Stories have on communities and children. Building on this, Skywoman Falling showed you how Creation Stories can impact and influence the daily lives and priorities of Indigenous Peoples. Lastly, you were able to engage with an Indigenous Elder, Mowegan, who shared traditional knowledges through the storytelling of the Story of Turtle Island. You should now have an understanding of the importance and value of Indigenous Creation stories.
