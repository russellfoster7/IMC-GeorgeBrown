Lesson 4: The Story of Turtle Island

Objectives

  • Explore the Story of Turtle Island and how it’s teachings are relevant to today
  • Understand the connection between the land, the environment, and industrialization
  • Explore how Westernized lifestyles differs from Mowegan’s lifestyle

The Story of Turtle Island

Document your responses to the questions to the right. Feel free to use any format to complete this exercise.

Reflection #1

Video Reflection

Card 1

What is the significance behind the name ’Turtle Island’?

What is the significance of the turtle being dispersed across the world map?

How do language and storytelling reflect the knowledge of its communities and peoples?

How do common food consumption practices today impact the land and its natural processes?

Reflection #2

Personal Reflection

Card 2

What main lesson do you take from The Story of Turtle Island?

After finishing the video, reflect on what gaps you experienced within the formal education system.

In what ways did the story of Turtle Island make you reflect on the way you live your day-to-day life? Is there anything you want to change after watching Mowegan’s video?

How does Mowegan’s way of communicating knowledge compare to others forms of knowledge transfer you have experienced?

Reflection #3

Making Connections

Card #3

What problems and solutions can the Teaching of the Bear point out about systemic discrimination and the government’s role in it?

How does Mowegan’s shared story relate to the environmental crises we see globally today?

How does industrialization relate to the Story of Turtle Island?
